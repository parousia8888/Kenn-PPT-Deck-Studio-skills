Etymology From Latin norma — a carpenter's square, the standard against which things are measured.
KEY CONCEPT

Normal distribution

正态分布
DEFINITION · n. A symmetric, bell-shaped spread of values where data clusters around the mean and thins out evenly toward both tails — fully described by just two numbers: its center (μ) and its spread (σ).
In plain words
Most results land near average; the further you go from the middle, the rarer they get.
You'll see it in
Heights, test scores, measurement error, daily temperatures.
