COMMON MISTAKES

Where this goes wrong

✕ MISCONCEPTION
“A bigger z-score always means a bigger raw value.”

Forgets that z can be negative — and depends on σ, not just x.

✓ CORRECT
A z-score measures distance from the mean in σ units — direction and scale both matter.

z = −2 is just as far out as z = +2.

✕
Forgetting to divide by σ. Subtracting the mean isn't enough — you must scale.
✕
Assuming every dataset is normal. Skewed data breaks the 68–95–99.7 rule.
