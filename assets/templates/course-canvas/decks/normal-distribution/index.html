STEP-BY-STEP

Turning a value into a z-score

  1. Find the centerWrite down the mean μ.
  2. Measure the gapSubtract the mean: x − μ.
  3. Scale by spreadDivide by the standard deviation σ.
  4. Read the resultz = how many σ from the mean.

IN-CLASS EXERCISE · 03

Try it on your own

4 MIN
A filling machine has μ = 1000 g and σ = 12 g. A bag weighing 976 g is 2 standard deviations below the mean.
z = (976 − 1000) / 12 = −2
TeacherLet them attempt before pressing reveal. Watch for students who forget to divide by σ.

RECAP — TODAY IN ONE PAGE

What to take away

HANDOUT
The normal curve is symmetric, set by the mean μ and spread σ.
The 68–95–99.7 rule tells you how much data sits within 1, 2, 3 σ.
A z-score rewrites a value as “how many σ from the mean”.
